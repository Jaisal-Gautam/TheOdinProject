Classic Margherita Pizza

Margherita Pizza is a classic Italian pizza that embodies simplicity and fresh flavors. Originating in Naples, it was named after Queen Margherita of Savoy in 1889. The pizza represents the colors of the Italian flag with its key ingredients: red tomato sauce, white mozzarella cheese, and green fresh basil. Its minimalist approach highlights the quality of the ingredients, making it a favorite for purists. Traditionally baked in a wood-fired oven, the Margherita pizza is a celebration of authentic Italian culinary artistry.

Ingredients:

  • Pizza dough
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 200g fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 475°F (245°C).
  2. Roll out the dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness.
  3. Spread the tomato sauce evenly on the dough.
  4. Place mozzarella slices on top and drizzle with olive oil.
  5. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.
  6. Garnish with fresh basil and serve!
